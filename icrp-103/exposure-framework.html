Situations, Types of Exposure, and Exposed Individuals - ICRP-103

üéØ Three Types of Exposure Situations
1. Planned Exposure Situations
"Situations involving the deliberate introduction and operation of radiation sources"
Examples:
  • Nuclear power plants
  • Medical facilities
  • Industrial radiography
  • Research reactors
  • Transport of radioactive material
Key Features: Can be planned and controlled in advance, optimization possible
2. Emergency Exposure Situations
"Situations that may occur during the operation of a planned situation, or from a malicious act, or from any other unexpected situation"
Examples:
  • Nuclear reactor accidents
  • Transport accidents
  • Radiological terrorism
  • Lost or stolen sources
  • Satellite re-entry
Key Features: Urgent action required, protective measures to reduce exposures
3. Existing Exposure Situations
"Exposure situations that already exist when a decision on the need for control has to be taken"
Examples:
  • Natural background radiation
  • Radon in buildings
  • NORM (naturally occurring radioactive material)
  • Residual contamination from past activities
  • Fallout from weapons testing
Key Features: Exposure already occurring, decide whether to intervene
‚ö° Types of Exposure
üìã Certain vs Potential Exposure

Certain Exposure: Exposure that is sure to be received

Potential Exposure: Exposure that might be received (probabilistic)

  • Must consider both in protection assessment
  • Potential exposure weighted by probability of occurrence
  • Important for accident scenarios and security considerations
üë∑ Occupational Exposure

Exposure of workers incurred as a result of their work

  • Scope: All work-related radiation exposure
  • Responsibility: Employer must ensure protection
  • Limits: 20 mSv/year effective dose
  • Optimization: Dose constraints typically 5-10 mSv/year
üè• Medical Exposure

Exposure of patients, volunteers in research, and persons helping patients

  • Patients: No dose limits (justification + optimization)
  • Research volunteers: Ethical approval + dose constraints
  • Carers/comforters: Guidance levels ~5 mSv per episode
  • Special consideration: Pregnant women and children
üë• Public Exposure

Exposure of members of the public from radiation sources

  • Excludes: Natural background, medical exposure as patients
  • Includes: Nuclear facilities, consumer products, occupationally exposed family members
  • Limits: 1 mSv/year effective dose
  • Optimization: Dose constraints typically 0.1-0.3 mSv/year

üéØ Key Exam Points

Situation Classifications:

  • Planned: Can optimize in advance
  • Emergency: Urgent protective actions
  • Existing: Decide whether to intervene

Individual Categories:

  • Workers: Accept higher doses for benefits
  • Public: Lower limits, no direct benefit
  • Trainees: Intermediate limits (learning)
  • Pregnant: Special fetal protection
Remember: The framework provides a systematic approach to radiation protection by first categorizing the situation, then the type of exposure, and finally the exposed individuals to determine appropriate protection measures.
