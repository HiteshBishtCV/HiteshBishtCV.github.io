üîÑ Conversion and Calibration

Fluence-to-Dose Conversion

Operational quantities are defined using fluence-to-dose conversion coefficients:

H*(d) = Œ¶ √ó h*(E, Œ±)
Where:
Œ¶ = Particle fluence
h*(E, Œ±) = Fluence-to-dose conversion coefficient
E = Energy, Œ± = Angle of incidence
Reference Radiation
Cs-137, Co-60, X-rays
Reference Phantom
ICRU sphere (30 cm)
Reference Geometry
Expanded parallel beam
Calibration Depth
10 mm (photons)
Key Calibration Point: Operational quantities are calibrated to give conservative estimates of protection quantities under reference conditions.

üö® Known Limitations of Operational Quantities

  • Energy dependence: Poor approximation at low photon energies (<30 keV)
  • Angular dependence: Overestimation at oblique incidence angles
  • Neutron energy: Significant variations for neutrons
  • Body size/shape: Based on standard phantom, not individual anatomy
  • Non-uniform exposure: Assumes uniform irradiation

üéØ Key Exam Points

Operational Quantity Purposes:

  • H*(10): Conservative estimate of E for external exposure
  • Hp(10): Individual effective dose assessment
  • H'(0.07) & Hp(0.07): Skin dose from beta radiation
  • Hp(3): Eye lens dose assessment

Key Depths to Remember:

  • 10 mm: Penetrating radiation (whole body)
  • 3 mm: Eye lens monitoring
  • 0.07 mm: Skin dose (beta particles)
  • ICRU sphere: 30 cm diameter phantom
Remember: Operational quantities are designed to be conservative overestimates of protection quantities under most exposure conditions, ensuring adequate protection while being practically measurable.
