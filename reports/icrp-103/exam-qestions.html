Scientific Rationale:

  • Beam geometry: Most occupational exposures are anterior-posterior or isotropic
  • Organ sensitivity: Proximity to lungs (wT = 0.12) and breast tissue (wT = 0.12)
Regulatory Requirements:
  • ICRP recommendation: Representative of dose to trunk
  • ISO standards: Specify chest level for personal dosimetry
  • National regulations: Usually specify 1.0-1.5m height range

Practical Implementation:

Standard Position: On the chest or upper torso, facing the radiation source, at a height representative of the maximum dose to the trunk (typically 1.0-1.5 meters above ground).
