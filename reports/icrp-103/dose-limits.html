üîç Key Exam Points

üìå Most Important Values to Remember:

  • Occupational effective dose: 20 mSv/year (average over 5 years)
  • Public effective dose: 1 mSv/year
  • Trainee effective dose: 6 mSv/year
  • Pregnant worker fetus: 1 mSv during pregnancy
  • Eye lens (occupational): 20 mSv/year (changed from 150 mSv in ICRP-60)

ICRP-60 vs ICRP-103

Major Change:

  • Eye lens limit reduced
  • ICRP-60: 150 mSv/year
  • ICRP-103: 20 mSv/year

Rationale for Reduction

  • New data on cataract induction
  • Lower threshold for cataracts
  • No-threshold model adopted
‚ö° Application of Dose Limits
Important Principles:
  • Effective dose limits: Apply to sum of doses from external exposure and committed effective dose from internal exposure
  • Equivalent dose limits: Apply to individual organs/tissues
  • 5-year averaging: Allowed for occupational effective dose (max 50 mSv in any single year)
  • ALARA principle: Doses should be as low as reasonably achievable below limits

üéØ Dose Constraint vs Dose Limit

Dose Limit: Regulatory limit that must not be exceeded

Dose Constraint: Upper bound of individual dose used in optimization (typically 0.1-0.3 mSv for public)
